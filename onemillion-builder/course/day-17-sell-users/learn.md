Day 17 is where the product becomes explainable and shareable. The app is live; now the learner turns it into a clear offer, a simple page, a pricing stance, and real outreach to first users.

## Learning Frame

- **Mental model:** selling is product learning. The first goal is not a huge launch; it is finding a small number of people who understand the pain and care enough to respond.
- **What can go wrong:** the learner writes vague copy, hides the CTA, overthinks pricing, or waits for a perfect product before talking to real people.
- **What to ignore today:** do not build a full marketing department. Create a clear first-user motion that can run today.

## What You Learn

- what brand means for an early product
- positioning and category choice
- benefit-first landing page copy
- CTA design
- pricing basics for MVPs
- launch content and outreach
- how to find communities without spamming
- feedback capture and signal interpretation
- simple SEO foundations and launch metrics

## Core Concepts

- **10 users who care are better than 1,000 who ignore you.** Day 17 is about specific users, not vanity reach.
- **Positioning tells people where to file the product in their brain.** Use: "For [target user] who [pain], [product] is a [category] that [benefit]. Unlike [alternative], we [differentiator]."
- **Good copy sells the outcome, not the feature.** A headline should say what gets better for the user.
- **Every page needs a CTA.** The user should know whether to try the product, join a waitlist, request a demo, email the builder, or give feedback.
- **Pricing is a learning tool.** Free beta, waitlist, one-time payment, subscription, and usage-based pricing are all valid if chosen intentionally.
- **Outreach is part of the build.** The product improves when real users react to it.
- **No response is signal if recorded honestly.** Track whether people are interested, confused, objecting, requesting features, or willing to pay.
- **SEO starts with clarity.** Title, description, OG tags, keywords, headings, and page structure should match the positioning.

## What You Produce

- product name, tagline, and one-line promise
- positioning statement
- landing/product page copy or live app page update
- simple pricing stance
- CTA
- launch posts for LinkedIn/X and one community-style post
- cold outreach messages
- list of 5-10 people or communities to contact
- feedback or pending-response status
- optional one-pager/deck only if the product needs it

## Human Decisions

- product name and tone
- category and differentiator
- target buyer/user
- CTA
- pricing stance
- who to contact first
- where to post without spamming
- which feedback changes the product, copy, or Builder Claim

## Done Checklist

- [ ] positioning statement is specific and benefit-first
- [ ] landing/product page has headline, pain, solution, proof/demo, CTA, and basic SEO metadata
- [ ] pricing stance is explicit, even if it is free beta
- [ ] outreach list includes 5-10 real people or relevant communities
- [ ] at least 3 direct outreach messages or posts are sent, or queued with a concrete send time
- [ ] every outreach message includes the live URL and a clear ask
- [ ] feedback signal or pending-response status is recorded honestly
- [ ] existing product copy, PRD, or Builder Claim notes are updated only if feedback changes direction
